The Constitution Was Not a Suggestion

Published March 3, 2026

Abraham Lincoln believed deeply in the rule of law.

During the most difficult crisis in American history, Lincoln often reminded the nation that its strength did not come from power or politics, but from its principles.

America was built on a framework designed to limit power and protect liberty: the Constitution.

It was not written as a set of suggestions. It was written as the foundation of the republic.

The Constitution defines what government may do, and just as importantly, what it may not do.

That framework is what allows freedom to exist.

Without it, power becomes arbitrary.

Without it, rights become temporary.

Without it, liberty becomes dependent on whoever happens to hold authority.

Lincoln understood this better than most. Even during war, he believed that the Constitution remained the anchor of American government.

That belief is the inspiration behind the design:

The Constitution Was Not a Suggestion.

The phrase is simple, but the idea behind it is enduring.

Principles only matter if they are upheld when they are inconvenient.

Wear the message.
